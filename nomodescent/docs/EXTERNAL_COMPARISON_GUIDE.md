# How To Compare Quotient Descent Against External Methods Honestly

Do not compare outputs at different epistemic levels as if they were the same
kind of result.

Rules:

- do not flatten an external method's assumptions
- do not compare an exact QD certificate to a heuristic baseline without
  saying so
- do not treat underdetermination as failure
- do not treat a sharper certificate as a broader domain claim
- do not silently upgrade a Gaussian-surrogate encoding into a theorem about
  the source-native law

When building an external comparison pack, record separately:

1. what was extracted exactly
2. what was encoded by modelling choice
3. what the external method actually certifies
4. what QD certifies exactly
5. what QD certifies only through audited approximation
6. whether the relation is:
   - reproduced
   - sharpened
   - distinguished
   - underdetermined under current evidence
   - incommensurate under current domain

The right outcome is often not "QD beats the baseline".

The right outcome is:

- the baseline and QD agree
- QD exposes a finer distinction
- or the current evidence is insufficient for an honest comparison
