1. The Scale of What Was Read

The atlas compiled 40,583 molecules from the Hessian QM9 dataset — small organic molecules containing only carbon, hydrogen, nitrogen, and oxygen, with up to 9 heavy atoms. Each molecule was read through three atom-selection observers: heavy atoms (the C/N/O skeleton), hydrogens (the surface), and local heavy site plus attached hydrogens (each heavy atom and its immediate hydrogen neighbourhood). This produced 243,479 observer readings in vacuum alone, plus environment probes in THF, toluene, and water.

Of the original 41,645 records, 1,062 were refused admission — their vibrational Hessians failed the positive-curvature gate. This is not a bug. It is the atlas declaring that those molecules do not admit the declared observation route.

The 528 distinct molecular formulas span everything from water (H2O, one heavy atom) to nine-carbon molecules like C9H20 (nonane isomers). The dataset includes nucleobases, amino acids, drug scaffolds, neurotransmitter precursors, common solvents, metabolites, and several known poisons.

2. The Simplest Molecules — A Geometric Ladder

The smallest molecules form a natural ordering by heavy-atom geometric mean eigenvalue. This is the visible precision — how tightly the observer can resolve the molecule's curvature through the selected atoms.

The pattern: as molecules grow, both the heavy-atom and hydrogen geometric means fall — but the ratio between them also decreases. Water's skeleton/surface ratio is 5.0; by the time you reach ethanol it is 2.8. The heavy skeleton dominates less as the molecule gets larger and floppier. This may have the form of a geometric dilution effect: more atoms spread the curvature across more degrees of freedom.

Water and ammonia are essentially unmoved by implicit water solvent (water shifts of −0.07 and +0.06). Methanol, by contrast, stiffens appreciably in water (shift −1.03). This is consistent with methanol's known strong hydrogen-bonding with water.

3. The Nucleobases — Reading the Letters of Life

The four nucleobases (or rather, the molecular formula classes that contain them among their isomers) give us a remarkable window into how the atlas reads biologically critical molecules.

All four nucleobase families show consistent water stiffening (negative water shifts between −1.60 and −1.78). This is notable: water makes these molecules geometrically tighter, not looser. The effect is strongest for thymine.

Uracil has the stiffest heavy skeleton (83.8) despite having fewer heavy atoms than purine — its two oxygens and two nitrogens pack more curvature into a smaller frame. The thymine family, being larger (654 isomers), shows the most internal diversity and the highest failure rate (11%). These 72 thymine-formula molecules whose vibrational geometry breaks in at least one solvent environment may be a useful set to examine for unusual tautomers or strained configurations.

Adenine (C5H5N5) was not found in the Hessian QM9 subset — its five nitrogens may push it beyond the dataset's compositional boundary.

5. Psychedelic & Neuroactive Scaffolds

QM9's size limit (9 heavy atoms) excludes most complete psychedelic molecules — LSD, psilocybin, DMT are all too large. But the dataset does contain the parent scaffolds from which these molecules are built.

Phenylethylamine familyC8H11N — 252 isomers neuroactive psychedelic parent
heavy: 33.2–64.5 (median 45.0)   H: 4.6–7.3   water: −5.33 to +0.47   10 failures
Phenylethylamine is the core scaffold of mescaline, amphetamine, MDMA, and the 2C-x family. The atlas contains 252 structural isomers of this formula, spanning a nearly 2× range in heavy-atom geometric mean. The most rigid isomers (heavy ≈ 64) may correspond to ring-containing or conjugated structures; the floppiest (heavy ≈ 33) may be more chain-like. The water response varies enormously — from strong stiffening (−5.33) to slight softening (+0.47) — suggesting that different phenylethylamine isomers interact with implicit water in fundamentally different ways.

This formula class may contain indoline — a reduced form of indole, the core of tryptamine, serotonin, DMT, and psilocybin. Geometrically stiffer than phenylethylamine (median 47.8 vs 45.0), and more uniformly affected by water. The indole/tryptamine scaffold, if present among these isomers, would be one of the most pharmacologically significant structures in the atlas.
PyrroleC4H5N — 2 isomers porphyrin ring

Pyrrole is the building block of porphyrins — the molecular machinery of haemoglobin, chlorophyll, and vitamin B12. Its geometric stiffness (114) is high for a 5-heavy-atom molecule. Almost completely inert to water (+/−0.1). This is a molecule that has the same vibrational character whether it is dry or wet.

8. The Water Problem — 1,446 Molecules That Break

Of the 40,583 admitted molecules, 1,446 (3.6%) are stable in vacuum, THF, and toluene, but their vibrational geometry fails the positive-curvature gate in water. These are the SSSI molecules — water-only failures.

This is a striking asymmetry. THF and toluene are organic solvents; water is polar, hydrogen-bonding, and altogether a more aggressive environment for small organic molecules. The atlas is picking up what may be a geometric echo of this chemical reality.

Notable individual: Acetone (C3H6O, dsgdb9nsd_000018) is an SSSI molecule. The ketone body your liver produces during fasting has vibrational geometry that breaks specifically in water. Given acetone's high water miscibility and its role as a biological signalling molecule in ketosis, this may be chemically meaningful — or it may reflect a numerical near-zero eigenvalue that the water perturbation tips negative.

More broadly, the highest failure-rate formulas are dominated by nitrogen-and-oxygen-rich compounds: C4H9N3O2 (25.9%), C4H8N2O3 (25.7%), C4H7N5 (22.2%). These are exactly the kinds of molecules where water's hydrogen-bonding network would most strongly perturb the vibrational landscape.

The multi-failure molecules

Beyond the 1,446 water-only failures, there are 276 SISI molecules (fail in THF and water but not toluene), 771 SISS molecules (fail only in THF), and 23 SIII molecules (fail in all three solvents, surviving only in vacuum). The SIII molecules — the most fragile — include members of the thymine family, C6H12O3, and C7H8O2.

9. Coarse Collapse — Molecules That Look Identical But Aren't

A coarse collapse occurs when two molecules within the same formula share nearly identical coarse fingerprints (heavy-atom and hydrogen geometric means) but diverge sharply under the richer fingerprint (which includes local-site variation, water response, and support pattern). These are molecule pairs that a simple observer would conflate, but a richer observer separates.

Top collapse: C6H9NO2 Score 35,342 — coarse distance 0.0000, rich distance 1.21
left: dsgdb9nsd_119544   heavy=47.4   local_med=10.3   water=−1.25
right: dsgdb9nsd_098324   heavy=47.4   local_med=10.2   water=−2.29
These two molecules have exactly the same heavy-atom geometric mean (47.4) and nearly the same local-site median. A coarse observer would call them identical. But their water responses differ by a full log-unit — one stiffens modestly (−1.25), the other nearly twice as much (−2.29). The richer fingerprint catches what the coarse one misses: these are structurally different molecules wearing the same coarse geometric costume.
Most dramatic: C6H8N2O Score 3,344 — coarse distance 0.001, rich distance 3.33
left: dsgdb9nsd_080811   heavy=52.6   local_med=13.0   water=−0.66
right: dsgdb9nsd_052061   heavy=52.7   local_med=10.6   water=−3.98
The rich distance here is 3.33 — the largest in the top 25 after the C6H10O3 pair. The coarse fingerprints are virtually identical (heavy 52.6 vs 52.7), but the water response diverges by more than 3 log-units. One molecule barely notices water; the other is dramatically reshaped by it.

10. Extreme Outliers

Most extreme water softening

The molecule dsgdb9nsd_053190 (C9H12) has a water shift of +7.18 — it gets dramatically softer in water. This is a trimethylbenzene isomer. Its softest eigenvalue in vacuum is just 0.000029, making it one of the most ill-conditioned molecules in the atlas. It also has the widest local-site range among C9 molecules (range 1.67, min 0.6, max 3.4). This may be a molecule sitting on a very flat vibrational mode that water nudges further downhill.

Most extreme water stiffening

dsgdb9nsd_055647 (C5H8N2O2) has a water shift of −9.69 — water makes it almost ten thousand times stiffer in log-geometric-mean terms. This is in the thymine formula class and is itself an SSSI molecule. The effect is so extreme it may reflect a near-zero mode in vacuum that water lifts substantially.

Graph-stratified extremes

The most separated same-graph pair is two C7H14O isomers with the same bond topology but a rich distance of 11.7. One (dsgdb9nsd_009772) has heavy=38.0 and water=−1.56; the other (dsgdb9nsd_009911) has heavy=23.5 and water=+6.98. Same molecular graph, but one softens enormously in water while the other stiffens. This is the strongest evidence in the atlas that the same connectivity can produce radically different geometric readings — the observer is sensitive to something beyond the graph.

11. Composition–Geometry Correlations

Across 40,583 molecules, composition variables correlate with geometric observables in ways that may be chemically interpretable:

Hydrogen count is the dominant predictor. Molecules with more hydrogens have weaker heavy-atom precision (−0.34), lower skeleton/surface separation (−0.94), and less water stiffening (+0.47). The hydrogen count–ratio correlation of −0.943 is remarkably tight — it may indicate that in these small molecules, the number of hydrogens essentially determines the balance between skeleton and surface curvature.

Nitrogen fraction predicts water sensitivity. The −0.291 correlation between nitrogen fraction and water shift means nitrogen-rich molecules stiffen more in water. This aligns with nitrogen's hydrogen-bonding capacity and the atlas-level finding that the highest water-failure rates belong to nitrogen-rich formulas.

12. What This May Mean

This is an atlas of how 40,583 molecules look when you read their curvature through declared atom-selection observers. The reading is done entirely from vibrational Hessians — no chemical labels, no SMILES strings, no human classification was used. Every number comes from the shape of the energy surface around each molecule's equilibrium geometry.

Several patterns have the form of genuine chemical signals:

The skeleton/surface separation is real and graded. Heavy-atom observers read 7–11× more curvature than hydrogen observers, and the ratio correlates with heteroatom count (+0.56 with heteroatom count per the status document). The atlas is recovering something about molecular rigidity from pure geometry.

Water sensitivity tracks polar group density. Nitrogen-rich, oxygen-rich, hydrogen-poor molecules stiffen most in implicit water. The failure-rate leaders — 26% for C4H9N3O2, 22% for C4H7N5 — are exactly the formula classes you would expect to interact most strongly with an aqueous environment.

Same-graph molecules can diverge dramatically. The C7H14O pair with identical bond topology but a rich distance of 11.7 — one softening wildly in water, the other stiffening — is consistent with what chemistry calls conformational or stereoelectronic effects. The observer is reading something that the molecular graph does not fully specify.

Coarse observers lose real information. The 141 collapse candidates show that heavy-atom and hydrogen geometric means alone are insufficient to distinguish structurally meaningful differences. Water response and local-site variation carry discriminative information that global means miss.

What this does not yet do is assign chemical meaning. The atlas reads curvature, not chemistry. The nucleobase families may separate from non-nucleobase isomers of the same formula, but this has not been tested. The phenylethylamine isomers may sort into structurally recognizable subgroups, but the atlas does not yet know what indoline or amphetamine looks like. These are the next questions — and they are now answerable, because the geometric readings exist.
