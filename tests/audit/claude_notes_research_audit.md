# Claude Notes Research Audit

Date: 2026-04-11

Source folder: `papers/claude_notes`.

## Executive Judgement

The Claude notes are useful as research inspiration, not as import-ready theory. They are strongest where they stay at covariance-level or supplied Fisher/Hessian geometry:

- visible precision as marginal precision in Gaussian/covariance form;
- clock as log-det covariance correlation;
- tower law for composed observers;
- closure leakage for supplied perturbation families;
- branch minimisation as an external lower-envelope principle.

They do not justify a generic non-Gaussian full-law selector. The notes mostly say this themselves. The real additions are narrower:

- an exact rank-one differential-correlation sector in a white/aligned background;
- a sharper two-rank formula showing where that sector fails in coloured backgrounds;
- a generic noncommuting closure obstruction;
- a residual-margin doctrine for branch/observer robustness;
- a local-geometry ensemble idea for higher-layer research.

Verdict: useful scoped analogy, no core-theory update.

### Neural Coding Note

Keep:

- Fisher information as a quadratic form in precision;
- Sherman-Morrison precision formula for covariance perturbations;
- rank-one exact sector when the covariance background is white or the relevant visible directions align;
- closure-adapted exactness for commuting/invariant families.

Amend:

- “leakage channel equals the signal direction” is theorem-grade only in the white/aligned sector. For a generic coloured covariance background, the hidden-gap increment is the difference of two rank-one terms and can have rank two.
- “closure-adapted eta=0 at all ranks” is not generic. It follows in the note because the family is constructed to commute. Generic noncommuting families need not have exact intermediate-rank closure.

Verdict: strong source for a precise differential-correlation appendix and a closure weakpoint.

Do not promote:

- anomalous dissipation derivations;
- identification of clock tails with anomalous exponents.

Verdict: useful as a higher-layer design prompt. The safe project statement is that a distribution over local Hessians carries tail-sensitive quadratic observables; it is not a full intermittency theory.

## New Checked Results

### Rank-One White Sector

For `Sigma_eps = I + eps f f^T`, with `f=(a,b)`, the hidden gap satisfies

```text
T - Phi =
  eps^2 ||b||^2 / ((1+eps||a||^2)(1+eps||f||^2)) * a a^T.
```

The follow-up numerics verified the formula to `5.03e-17`, with rank one.

### Coloured Background Two-Rank Boundary

For `Sigma_eps = Sigma_0 + eps f f^T`, define `H_0=Sigma_0^{-1}`, `Phi_0=(Sigma_0)_{VV}^{-1}`, `u_V=(H_0 f)_V`, and `w_V=Phi_0 f_V`. Then

```text
Delta R =
  eps/(1+eps f_V^T Phi_0 f_V) * w_V w_V^T
  - eps/(1+eps f^T H_0 f) * u_V u_V^T.
```

Thus `rank(Delta R) <= 2`. The one-channel result requires collinearity of `u_V` and `w_V`. The follow-up numerics found a rank-two example with formula error `1.18e-16`.

### Generic Noncommuting Closure Obstruction

Take `D_1=diag(1,...,n)` and generic symmetric `D_2`. Since `D_1` has simple spectrum, exact closure for `D_1` forces the observer subspace to be a coordinate eigenspan. Exact closure for `D_2` then requires a zero cross-block of `D_2`, a measure-zero condition.

The follow-up numerics used `n=6`, `m=2`, commutator norm `6.80`, and smallest coordinate cross-block certificate `1.113`; exact common rank-2 closure was absent.

### Residual Margin

If a quadratic branch gap is `gamma` and law/model residuals are bounded by `R`, ordering is certified only when `gamma > 2R`. This is the correct practical bridge from quadratic selection to fuller laws.

The follow-up numerics used `gamma=0.08`. With `R=0.03`, reversal is impossible. With `R=0.05`, a constructive reversal exists and random residuals reversed the verdict `953/50000` times.

### Local-Geometry Ensembles

The turbulence “distribution over local observer geometries” idea is real but should be formalised as an ensemble layer:

```text
H_local ~ mu, then study distributions of Phi_C(H_local), Lambda(H_local), tau(H_local).
```

The script constructed two matched-mean local-coupling ensembles. Both had mean coupling `0.13315`; the clock at mean coupling was `0.02363`, while mean clocks were `0.03734` and `0.03455`, with 99th percentile clocks `0.38274` and `0.48870`.

This proves the basic warning: one averaged Hessian can miss tail-sensitive observation structure.
